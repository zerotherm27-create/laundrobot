Executive Summary

LaundroBot is a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform built specifically for laundry businesses in the Philippines. It replaces the manual, error-prone workflows that most Philippine laundry shops rely on today — paper logbooks, WhatsApp order tracking, and a part-time Messenger encoder — with an automated, AI-powered system that works 24 hours a day.

The product is live. It accepts bookings natively through Facebook Messenger using a webform that appears inside the chat (no app download required), manages orders through a Kanban board, processes payments through Xendit, and runs an AI chatbot powered by Google Gemini 2.5 Flash that responds to customer inquiries in Tagalog and English at any hour.

The core insight: The Philippines has 61.8 million Facebook Messenger users — 90% of all internet users. Filipino customers already message laundry shops on Messenger to ask about rates. LaundroBot turns that conversation into a booking, a payment, and an order — automatically.

Problem Statement

Philippine laundry shops are overwhelmingly micro-enterprises. The PSA counted 1,893 formal establishments in 2020, but the real total including informal operators is estimated at 10,000+. The vast majority operate without any dedicated business software.

How most shops operate today

❌ Manual order tracking

Orders are written in paper logbooks or tracked in WhatsApp threads. Weight, service type, and special instructions are logged by hand. Disputes over lost or damaged items are common because there is no digital record.

❌ Human Messenger encoder

Many shops hire a part-time staff member whose sole job is to reply to Messenger inquiries. At ₱7,000/month, this person works 8am–5pm — leaving evening and overnight inquiries unanswered and orders lost.

❌ Cash-only payment leakage

Payment is collected at pickup. Customers who do not return on time cause cash flow gaps. There is no system to send reminders or automatically cancel unclaimed orders, so shops absorb the loss.

❌ No customer notifications

Customers must call or text to check if their laundry is ready. This creates unnecessary back-and-forth and ties up the shop owner's personal phone number for business communication.

The cost of the status quo: A shop hiring a part-time Messenger encoder (₱7,000/month), plus losing 2–3 orders per week to missed evening inquiries (₱200–400/order), is effectively spending ₱10,000–15,000/month on a problem LaundroBot solves for ₱599–1,999/month.

The Solution

LaundroBot is a complete operating system for a Philippine laundry shop. It is designed around one core insight: Filipino customers already use Facebook Messenger to interact with businesses. Instead of forcing them to download an app or visit a website, LaundroBot brings the entire booking and payment flow inside Messenger.

How it works for the customer

  1. Customer sends a message to the laundry shop's Facebook Page
  2. The LaundroBot AI chatbot responds instantly — in Tagalog, English, or Taglish — answering questions about rates, schedules, and services
  3. Customer taps "Book Now" in the persistent menu
  4. A full booking webform slides up inside Messenger (no app download, no new account)
  5. Customer selects service, weight, pickup address, and schedule — and pays via Xendit
  6. Order lands in the shop's Kanban board. Shop owner and customer are both notified at every status change.

How it works for the shop owner

  • Kanban board — drag orders through: New → Confirmed → In Progress → Ready → Completed
  • Walk-in POS — create orders manually for walk-in customers with the same pricing engine
  • Blast messaging — send promotions or updates to all customers in one click (Growth plan)
  • Auto-reminders — 4-stage payment reminders at 1hr, 3hr, 6hr, and 23hr post-order; auto-cancel at 24hr
  • Revenue reports — daily/weekly/monthly revenue summaries by service type

Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1: Direct outreach (Months 1–3)

  • Identify laundry shops in Metro Manila, Cebu, and Davao with active Facebook Pages
  • Send personalised DMs demonstrating the product (short Loom video showing the Messenger booking flow)
  • Offer a 30-day free trial with assisted setup included
  • Target: 10–20 paying tenants by end of Month 3

Phase 2: Community & referrals (Months 4–9)

  • Facebook Groups: Philippine laundry business Facebook groups have 50,000–150,000 members. Post case studies, tutorials, and offer-limited spots
  • Referral program: Existing tenants get 1 free month per successful referral
  • Content marketing: "How [Shop Name] saves ₱7,000/month with LaundroBot" — real testimonials from beta users
  • Target: 50 paying tenants by Month 9

Phase 3: Inbound & partnerships (Month 10+)

  • SEO: Target "laundry management software Philippines," "laundry booking system Philippines" — zero competition currently
  • Google Ads: Low CPC in this niche; target shop owners actively searching for solutions
  • Franchise partnerships: Reach out to laundry franchise groups (Labamax, etc.) for bulk licensing
  • YouTube / TikTok: Tutorial content for laundry shop owners in Tagalog
  • Target: 150+ tenants by end of Year 1, 300 by end of Year 2

Onboarding & retention

  • Assisted setup call included for all plans (30–60 minutes via video call)
  • In-app guided setup checklist (services → delivery zones → Messenger connection)
  • Monthly check-in email with usage summary and tips
  • Target churn rate: <5%/month (industry average for B2B SaaS is 3–7%)

LaundroBot's sustainable competitive advantages

  • Messenger-native booking — the only laundry software that lets customers book inside Messenger without leaving the app
  • Tagalog AI chatbot — tuned specifically for Philippine laundry conversations; not a generic chatbot
  • Zero transaction fee — unlike marketplaces (SWOSH, Lalaba) that take a cut, LaundroBot takes nothing from payments, making it clearly aligned with shop owners' interests
